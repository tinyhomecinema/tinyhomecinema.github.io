THE TINY HOME CINEMA
The Tiny Home Cinema (or "Cineminha", as it is affectionately called by its owners) was built in a room of just 6.72 m2 (72.33 sq ft) in a small 3 bedroom apartment located in Campinas, Brazil, with an approximate total cost of R$ 34,200 (about US$ 6,500 at the time it was built). It has two seats, with an approximate viewing distance of 2 meters (6.5 feet) from a 55" screen and features a 5.1.2 spatial sound system. Its illumination system has two sets of lights, one cold (primary ceiling spots) and one warm (secondary dimmable back wall sconces and front ceiling spots), providing two different ambiances according to how room is being used. It has an automation system which makes possible to control all electronic devices and room illumination, including syncronization of film play/pause with lights off/on. An air conditioner provides cool and heat climatization options. With walls painted in a dark tone to avoid reflections from screen light, alongside with black furnitures and a blackout curtain, provides a very immersive visual experience, even during daylight times. It is fully decorated with film posters, theater signs, movie memorabilia, books and posters with famous film quotes. A collection of a few hundred titles of Blu-ray discs, DVDs and soundtrack CDs completes the film loving experience.
